# Chronogears Showdown 
## Game Concept 
In this mini-game, players engage in a tabletop challenge where they compete against an in-game NPC or each other to simulate the process of creating and maintaining clockwork mechanisms. They use dice and cards to assemble their clockwork devices.

## Materials Needed 
- A set of polyhedral dice (d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20) for each player.
- A deck of standard playing cards.

## How to Play 
Each player is dealt a hand of five cards from the deck. These cards represent clockwork parts, with the card value (Ace to King) representing the part's complexity and power. The suits could represent different types of parts: Hearts for energy sources, Diamonds for gears, Clubs for protective casings, and Spades for control mechanisms.

Each player then takes turns placing down a card, representing the installation of a part in their clockwork device. Players roll a D20 and must roll a number equal to or greater than the card value to successfully install the part (Ace is 1, Jack is 11, Queen is 12, King is 13).

If a player rolls less than the required value, they fail to install the part. The card is discarded, and the player must wait until their next turn to try again with a new card.

A player may choose to "upgrade" an existing part by playing a card of the same suit but higher value. They must then roll two dice:
the D20 to attempt the installation, as normal, and the D12. If the D12 roll is lower than the value of the existing card, the upgrade is successful. If not, both the existing card and the upgrade are discarded.

Once all players have played all their cards, the values of the successfully installed parts are totaled. The player with the highest total has built the most efficient clockwork device and wins the round. 

To add strategy to the game, consider adding special rules. For example, a player who places down a King can force another player to discard a card, or a player who places down an Ace can swap a card with another player.

This mini-game requires strategic thinking and a bit of luck, making it an engaging way to simulate the complexities of clockwork construction in a simplified form. It's also versatile and could be played virtually anywhere, as long as players have access to dice and a deck of cards.
