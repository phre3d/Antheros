# Artificer's Library

>[!infobox]
>![[plots/clockwork-tower/images/clockwork-tower-header.jpg]]
>
>---
>

This grand room with high ceilings and a damaged roof houses the Clockwork Tower's wealth of knowledge. However, adventurers must tread carefully as the Archives are protected by Cogsprocket Spiders, small constructs tasked with repairing and maintaining the historical records which are not particularly fond of intruders.

> [!read-aloud] **Read Aloud**
 >  The room you've entered has a grandeur about it; tall ceilings stretch high above you, bearing evidence of past opulence, now marred by wear and damage over time. Great bookshelves appear throughout the room, though many are toppled or in disarray. Intricate cogs and gears are embedded in the design of the room, especially overhead along the ceiling where there is a set of tracks and mechanisms above the shelving. At several locations the tracks are borken or misaligned.
 >
 > Curiously, the air is filled with a subtle rhythmic ticking, punctuated by the skittering sound of many legs. As you venture further, you spot mechanical spiders moving among the shelves, their crystalline eyes focused on their tasks or turning toward you with wary caution.

In the Artificer's Library, employ the Cogsprocket Spiders for intricate combat dynamics. Their repair capabilities present novel hurdles as players grapple with foes that mend themselves continually. The towering bookshelves serve multiple purposes: as cover, climbing aids, or hiding spots. When introducing larger constructs like the Cogsprocket Sentinel or Cogsprocket Weaver, think about enhancing their skill sets to harmonize with the smaller arachnid mechanisms. It's vital to understand that these spiders prioritize the Library's upkeep above all; they are guardians, not killers.

Within the Artificer's Library lies the Chronos Codex, an artifact of unparalleled significance. This tome stands out not just for its rich historical content but also for its powerful magical attributes. Nestled deep within the library's core, the Codex offers wielders immense power and holds the potential solutions to rectify the temporal distortions plaguing both the Clockwork Tower and the lands that surround it.

# Books found in the library

###  **Chronosmith's Compendium of Clockwork Arcana**  

 This hefty, leather-bound tome has a cover adorned with intricate etchings of gears and runes. It was penned by [[plots/clockwork-tower/npcs/constantine-chronosmith|Constantine Chronosmith]] himself and contains a wealth of knowledge on clockwork devices and temporal magic. Flipping through the pages, adventurers can find a section dedicated to the Chronos Resonator. It not only describes the Resonator's function but also lists its essential components, making references to the Crank of Quickening, Echoing Cog, and Temporal Fragment. The book also provides clues on proper alignment techniques for these components. A character who spends 1 hour reading this section can make one Arcana check with advantage when attempting to restore the Chronos Resonator.

###  **Temporal Fragments: A Study in Theoretical Arcanodynamics**  

 This scroll is stored in a decorative, cylindrical container that looks much more recent than the surrounding volumes. It's a highly technical paper, filled with mathematical equations and magical symbols. At the heart of it, the paper theorizes on the nature of "temporal fragments," which are essential components of devices that manipulate time, such as the Chronos Resonator. A character who takes time to decipher the scroll (<u>requires a DC 15 Arcana check and 30 minutes of concentration</u>) gains a unique insight into the Temporal Fragment needed for the Chronos Resonator. This understanding grants a one-time advantage on the DC 20 Arcana check required to align the Temporal Fragment with the Temporal Core of the Resonator.

###  **Guardians of Gear and Cog: A History of Clockwork Constructs**  

  This historical account describes the origins and advancements in clockwork constructs throughout the ages. It delves into the various types of constructs, their purposes, and their roles in society, from simple clockwork toys to advanced Clockwork Sentinels. Illustrations pepper the pages, showing the intricate designs of these mechanical wonders. A character who spends 2 hours reading this book gains advantage on History or Arcana checks related to clockwork constructs for the next 7 days.

###  **Celestial Orbits and Temporal Flux**  

 The Alchemy of Time: A scholarly tome filled with complex diagrams and charts mapping the flow of celestial bodies and their potential influence on the passage of time. It's a challenging read, laden with advanced magical theories that ponder the relationship between the heavens and time itself. Characters who successfully read and comprehend this book (requires a <u>DC 18 Arcana check and 2 hours of study</u>) can use the celestial patterns described within to gain a one-time reroll for any failed saving throw against spells or effects that manipulate time.

###  **The Pocket Guide to Arcane Sigils**  

 This small, pocket-sized book offers a comprehensive overview of commonly used arcane symbols, glyphs, and runes. While it is designed to be more of a reference guide than a scholarly text, it provides valuable information for anyone working with arcane arts, including brief notes on potential uses or misuses of each symbol. Characters who spend at least 30 minutes skimming through this guide can add +1 to any single Arcana check related to identifying arcane symbols for the next 24 hours.

# The Chronos Codex

The Chronos Codex is a legendary artifact, not merely for its historical value but also for its potent arcane properties. Located at the  heart of the Artificer's Library, it promises both power and solutions to the time anomalies afflicting the Clockwork Tower and its surroundings.

The Codex can be found upon an ornate pedestal, enclosed by a 10-foot radius protective barrier of swirling time energy.  If the Codex is disturbed without disabling the defenses (see below), a  **a Cogsprocket Sentinel**  emerge from hidden compartments to defend the Codex (one each time the codex is disturbed).

 *When they first approach the force shield:*

> [!read-aloud] **Read Aloud**
 >  
 >
 > Looking through the swirling field of energy you can see a large book on a lectern.

## Chronos Codex Defences

### 1) Temporal Vortex

 Any creature that steps within 10 feet of the Codex without deactivating the protection suffers from a localized time distortion. They must succeed on a <u>DC 15 Constitution saving throw</u> or be affected by the Slow spell for 1 minute. Meanwhile, the  **Cogsprocket Sentinels**  are alerted by the disturbance of the barrier and activate to defend the Codex.

The barrier can be deactivated with an <u>Intelligence (Arcana) check or a straight Intelligence check (using tinker's tools) at DC 17</u>. Finishing and repairing the functional issues of the library also work to weaken the barrier. Successfully completing both tasks deactivates the Temporal Vortex barrier entirely.

 **Success:**  The vortex disappears, allowing physical access to the Codex.

 *When that can see more and/or touch the book:*

> [!read-aloud] **Read Aloud**

If anyone asks, the lines and symbols mimic those on the platform in one corner of the room.

### 3) Memory Ward  

Those who attempt to read the Codex without disabling the Mnemosyne ward will have their memories of the Codex's contents erased after 1 hour. The ward can be disabled with a succesful <u>DC 16 Intelligence (Arcana) check</u>.

## Deciphering the Codex  

The pages are encrypted. <u>Decoding requires an Intelligence (Arcana) check</u>.- **DC 14:**  Takes 1 hour to decipher a page.

- **DC 17:**  Takes 10 minutes.
- **DC 22:**  Almost instant comprehension.

## Importance for the PCs

 **Restoring Balance:**  The temporal anomalies affecting the Clockwork Tower and the surrounding area of Haven can be cured using a ritual from the Codex. The ritual requires:- 1 hour of uninterrupted casting.

- At least three casters proficient in Arcana.
- Expending a spell slot of 5th level or higher.

 **Personal Stakes:**  The Codex has the potential to restore a PC suffering from a temporal curse. This would require a caster to make a successful <u>DC 22 Intelligence (Arcana) check</u> and expend a spell slot of 4th level or higher in order to tap into the Codex's power to reverse time-bound ailments.

 **Power and Knowledge:**  Arcane casters who study the Codex for 8 hours and succeed on a <u>DC 25 Intelligence (Arcana) check</u>  can temporarily add the spells <u>Time Stop</u> and <u>Haste</u> to their spellbooks or list of known spells for the next 7 days. After 7 days, the knowledge fades, and the spell must be re-learned from the Codex.

# Incomplete Work and Wear & Tear

As the adventurers navigate through the grandeur and decay of the Artificer's Library, the incomplete projects and wear and tear that Constantine mentioned becomes evident. These manifestations challenge the party, but also provide an opportunity for them to make tangible progress in stabilizing the Clockwork Tower.

## Incomplete Work: The Time-Telling Totems

- **Task:**  On one corner of the library, stands an array of six totem-like pillars, each with intricate carvings of different celestial bodies. A close inspection (<u>DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check</u>) reveals that these totems are mechanisms meant to keep track of the positions of the celestial bodies.  However, two of the totems are incomplete
 - Ebla,
 - Tabit
. Symbols on the other four correspond to:
 - Antheros (green),
 - Celene (yellow)
 - Hasp (blue), and
 - the sun (red).
- **Retrieve the parts:**   Hidden among the library shelves, adventurers can find two intricately carved pieces that fit the two broken  totems. A <u>DC 17 Wisdom (Perception)</u> or a <u>DC 14 Intelligence (Investigation)</u> check will help locate these carvings.
- **Assembly:**  Once retrieved, a <u>DC 16 Intelligence (Arcana) check</u> or a <u>DC 14 Dexterity check (can use tinker's tools to gain advantage)</u> will successfully attach the carvings to the totems. Once all totems are complete, they synchronize and slide into relative positions aligning to the current positions of their corresponding celestial bodies. There is also a soft glow that surrounds the Chronos Codex, indicating a weakening of its Temporal Lock (see above).

## Wear and Tear: Decaying Book Retrieval System

The library once had an ingenious mechanism to fetch books from high shelves: a series of pulleys, ropes, and mechanical claws. However, over time, these have worn out, making many books inaccessible.

 **Repairing the mechanism:**

Players can use their skills to mend broken ropes, realign pulleys, and recalibrate mechanical claws. This requires:

- <u>DC 10 Intelligence check (using tinker's tools)</u> to mend the broken ropes.
- <u>DC 16 Strength (Athletics)</u> check to realign the mechanism's heavy pulleys.
- <u>DC 15 Intelligence check (using tinker's tools)</u> to recalibrate the mechanical claws.
- A total of three successful checks stabilizes the mechanism.

Once operational, the system can be used to retrieve a unique tome, Mechanisms of the Clockwork Realm, from a top shelf. This book provides invaluable insights into the workings of the Clockwork Tower and provides the reader a +2 bonus on skill checks related to understanding or repairing mechanisms within the tower.

While the adventurers are working on these tasks, the  **Cogsprocket Spiders**  might perceive this as tampering and become more aggressive. Alternatively, if players can communicate their intentions (perhaps using a spell or ability to speak with constructs or presenting the tools in a non-threatening manner), the spiders might even assist, or at least refrain from hindering the party's efforts.

Lastly, repairing these mechanisms and completing the unfinished work will not only aid in accessing the Chronos Codex but also stabilize a part of the Clockwork Tower, fulfilling Constantine's request and bringing the adventurers one step closer to rectifying the tower's anomalies.

# Environmental Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the Library takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects:

1. **Clockwork Ambush:**  A hidden compartment in one of the bookshelves slides open, releasing an additional 2 Cogsprocket Spiders.  The spiders are focused on their repair tasks and are less aggressive - the won't attack unless threatened directly.
2. **Temporal Distortion:**  A device in the room momentarily distorts time. All creatures except the spiders have their speed halved until the next round.
3. **Overdrive:**  All Cogsprocket Spiders and their variants gain an additional reaction until the end of the next round.
4. **Mechanical Malfunction:**  Random gears and cogs in the room spin out of control, creating difficult terrain in a 15-foot radius from a random point in the room.
