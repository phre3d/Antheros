# Outline
The late Lieutenant [[plots/murder-at-the-old-wolf-inn/npcs/artemis-nahanna|Artemis Nahanna]]'s 
room (Area 12) is located upstairs. The 
room is the finest room at the Old Wolf 
Inn, and the fireplace is larger than 
in all the other guest rooms. (See [[plots/murder-at-the-old-wolf-inn/rooms-at-the-old-wolf-inn|Rooms at the Old Wolf Inn]] for a 
map of the Old Wolf Inn.)

The door (AC 15, HP 12) to the room is locked 
with a sturdy iron lock. The lock requires a 
successful DC 13 Dexterity (Thieves' Tools) check 
to pick.

Inga has the key to this room and will unlock 
the door if the characters ask her. She will also 
tell them that the door was locked when they 
discovered the dead body, and that no one has 
touched the body.

> [!read-aloud] **Read Aloud** 
 >  
 > As you enter the fine guest room, a shiver 
dances down your spine. 

Your eyes are drawn to the unnaturally still 
shape of a male elf, who lies face down on 
the wide double bed. There are no immediate 
signs of struggle or violence.

The room is dark, and the air is chilly. 
Outside, the wind howls like an angry wolf, 
and cascades of rain hammer without 
remorse on the roof.

The walls in this guest room are painted sage 
green and feature a golden-painted picture rail. 
Two empty candlestick holders, made of brushed 
iron and wrought in traditional hexagonal 
dwarven style, are fitted on one wall. A large chest 
stands near a writing desk. 
On either side of the 
bed are two bedside tables. On one of them is a 
wooden mug. 

## investigating the body 
Lieutenant [[plots/murder-at-the-old-wolf-inn/npcs/artemis-nahanna|Artemis Nahanna]] is clearly dead. His 
skin is discoloured, and his face is a ghastly mix 
of grey and blue hues. He wears a Winterhold 
Guards' uniform but no armour. In his pockets are 
a key and a leather drawstring pouch. The key has 
a wooden tag engraved with the number 3 and a 
curious-looking badger. 

The wooden tag matches the sign on the door to 
this room, and the key fits the lock to the door. The 
drawstring pouch contains 17 gp, 3 sp, and 1 cp.

 **--> On a closer look, DC 13 Wisdom (Medicine):** 
As you investigate the body more closely, you find 
that the victim appears to have died of unnatural 
causes. Due to the discolouring of the deceased's 
skin, it is hard to establish the true cause of death, 
but he appears to have been either poisoned or 
strangled. You also notice several greenish bruises 
along the victim's ribcage, which looks like the 
result of one or two broken ribs. 

The discolouration of Artemis' face is a result 
of the poison that he drank before he died. The 
bruises stem from when Artemis fell off his horse 
during Jon's first murder attempt. 

# searching the crime scene  
This room hides numerous clues that are vital to 
the investigation, so the characters will do well to 
investigate it thoroughly.

## looking around the room 
 **--> On a closer look, DC 8 Wis (Perception):**  You 
notice that one of the windows is slightly ajar. 

The window was opened by Inga after she 
discovered the body. According to a common 
tradition in Eastern Farraway, opening a window 
in a room where a person has passed away can 
help their soul depart to the afterlife. 

## searching the fireplace 
 **--> On a closer look, DC 12 Intelligence 
(Investigation):**  As you poke through the cold and 
charred logs in the open fireplace, you notice that 
some of the ash flakes resemble torn sheets of 
paper. On a few of the pieces are faint traces of 
unreadable handwriting.

The burnt pieces of paper are the first draft of 
the report that Artemis was working on during 
previous evening. Artemis later wrote the full 
report, but the pages were later stolen by Jon 
Callion after his death. 

## investigating the bedside tables 
In one of the drawers is a small wooden box. 
Inside the box is a golden locket with an 
exquisitely painted miniature portrait of a female 
half-elf woman with long, silvery hair in a noble 
gown. The locket rests on a small deep purple 
velvet cushion.

The painted picture is a portrait of Artemis' 
sister, Thanelïa Nahanna.

 **--> On a closer look, DC 13 Wisdom (Survival) 
or Intelligence (Nature):**  You find that the wooden 
mug on the bedside table is empty, but you notice 
the faint scents of peppermint and fennel root 
upon it.

The mug contained peppermint and fennel tea 
with honey that Artemis ordered from Inga after 
dinner when the frog leaves started to affect him 
and he felt queasy.

## looking through the chest 
The chest contains Artemis' well-polished armour 
and weapons, as well as a selection of clean and 
neatly folded clothes. There is also a bag of dirty 
laundry, a shaving kit, a steel mirror, a shoe brush 
set, and a few other mundane items.

 **--> On a closer look, DC 10 Intelligence 
(Investigation):**  You find an empty glass vial 
labelled “Potion of Healing” that has slid down 
between the clothes. You also discover a uniform 
jacket with mud stains in the laundry bag.

The empty vial was a bottle of poison, but Jon 
changed the labels and gave the vial to Artemis, 
who drank the lethal poison in the belief that it 
was a healing potion. There are no traces of the 
poison left. 

The uniform jacket was worn by Artemis during his 
riding accident when he fell into a puddle of mud.

## investigating the writing desk 
The wax candles in the candlestick holders on the 
elegant writing desk have burnt down to stumps. 
On the desk is an elegant letter opener in silver, 
a pair of quills, a small jar of black ink, and a tiny
pile of shavings that appears to be the result of 
someone sharpening the quills more than once. 

The desk's drawer contains several papers 
with a report written in Common. The report 
is unfinished and concerns a recent military 
scouting mission conducted by Artemis. The 
handwriting is initially neat, but it degrades 
drastically towards the end of the text. It is 
impossible to tell if this is because the writer was 
tired or feeling unwell.

 **--> On a closer look, DC 15 Intelligence 
(Investigation):**  As you skim the report, you realise 
that several pages seem to be missing.

The report was written by Artemis. It contained 
detailed information about his discovery of the cave 
that the drow use, which is the reason behind why 
Jon decided to kill him. After the murder, Jon stole 
the pages with the most sensitive information.
