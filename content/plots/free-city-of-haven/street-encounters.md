# FCoH Street Encounters

# 2) Boy Fight

As the group strolls down the street, two young boys start fighting about 10' in front of them. At the height of the conflict, a third urchin will attempt to cut an item of value from a randomly selected member of the party and make off with it.

The young thief, [[people/vechin|Vechin]] (#901) has a pickpocketing chance of 45%, but he is only 1st level. If the thief succeeds, the character will lose some valuable-appearing item worn outside his clothing (purse, scabbard, pouch, etc.).

If the boy is noticed, he will run into, and down, an alley less than 20' away (the trio of boys stages their operation carefully) and disappear over a wooden fence. It requires CON check (DC 12) to catch the boy. While the thief is being chased, the other two will, of course, also disappear.  

# 3) Guide

A young street rat (sex indeterminable, due to dirt) approaches the party, and suggests that they employ the youngster as a guide, for a silver piece.

If the party does not hire the urchin, they will follow the party for 2 blocks, hurling epithets and mud, garbage, etc. (unless chased away). If the party does hire the kid, there is an 80% chance that they know the location if it is close by, 50% if it is within a reasonably local area, and 10% if it is "somewhere in the city*". In cases of lack of knowledge, the urchin will lead the party in circles, until the group grows tired and drives the child off.

# 4) Shell Game

Standing at the side of the way is a man, with a small felt covered table in front of him. On the table are three silver cups, one of which conceals a little golden ball. The table has been strategically placed near the entrance to an alley. The man is rallying the crowd of (2d10) watchers around him to

"Try your ability! Is the hand really faster than the eye? Any amount from a bit to a gold piece! Can you find the little gold ball?"

The huckster is running the old shell game, and he is good! If a character elects to play, the following method works well.

1. The ball is displayed under a given cup, A, B, or C.
2. The huckster then makes 3d10 moves.
3. Roll d100:
	- 01-20, the ball is under cup A.
	- On 21-40, cup B.
	- On 41-60, cup C.
	- And on 61-00, it is concealed in his hand. If the characters demand to see the location of the ball, the huckster will slip it under the cup he is lifting – Perception roll (plus Sleight of Hand bonus) vs. DC 21 to see him do it

If he should be caught cheating, he will immediately run off, leaving the table, cups, and ball (the equipment has a total worth of about 2gp) for whoever wishes to claim it.

Note: The original formula to detect cheating ends up at approx. 85% chance of huckster doing it unnoticed: (110 + # of moves) - (DEX + WIS + INT of highest character)

# 5) Centaur Cab

A centaur cab comes pell-mell down the street at full speed. The driver/motive power is yelling at the top of his lungs. The other pedestrians scatter quickly. The cab rattles quickly past the party.

All characters in the group should DEX check vs. DC 15 to avoid being thoroughly splashed with muddy water and garbage. If the check is missed by 1/3 of the character's DEX or more, the character has been struck by the careening cab and suffers 2d4 damage 2d4. If maximum damage is rolled, a CON check (DC 12) is required to avoid a broken bone (d10): 1-3 = arm, 4-8 = 1d6 ribs, 9-10 = leg.

# 6) Traffic Jam

Some fifteen cursing drayers, cabbies, and riders are trying to untangle their animals and vehicles from a near pile-up which blocks the near-by intersection completely. One of the wagons appears to be loaded with small parcels, but no one seems to be paying particular attention to it.

If characters wish to attempt removal of the parcels, a DEX check (DC 10) is required for each package to be removed unnoticed. The packages each contain quality merchandise (small objects d'art, less costly jewelry, bric-a-brac) worth 1d10 x 1d10 gp each.

# 7) Caravan

  * (Happens only on Caravan Street, otherwise no encounter)*  
A colorful (and relatively noisy) caravan is passing. A caravan consists of traders, animals, wagons, guards, and travelers. The animals will be (d10): 1-3 = horses; 4-9 = mules, donkeys, or burros; 10 = camels. The caravan is (d10): 1-3 = small; 4-8 = medium; 9-10 = large.

# 8) Land Scam

A booth with a colorful awning has been set up along the road. It is decorated with bright banners and streamers, and a man out front is bellowing about great bargains in land for those who "have always wanted to own the land that could feed them handsomely." Inside the booth's tent-like structure may be seen a desk, with a second man seated behind it.

These two entrepreneurs are selling bargain plots of land "in the beautiful foothills of the Mountains of Peace", about 250 miles north and slightly east of Haven. The land is there, and the titles are good, but the parcels will be infested with unfriendly orcs, who have been driven out of the Peace Mountains by the hordes of Lizarim which have moved in. The costs are set at only 10 gp an acre, a most reasonable price.

# 9) Rats

A display of trained rats is occurring at a nearby intersection. The rodents are being put through a series of tricks and stunts by a brightly dressed man, who rewards each successful performance with a small piece of cheese from his pocket. Nearby is parked a colorfully painted caravan (a gypsy style wagon-house), from which an assistant occasionally appears to deposit a fresh cage of rats, and retrieve another.

These rats are very well-trained thieves as well as performers. There is a 15% chance that one of the rats will attempt to steal a small item from a random member of the party. It requires a saving roll against 1/4 (INT + WIS) for the character to notice the theft attempt. Otherwise, he will lose a coin, a gem, or a small item that is loosely attached. The rats return to the wagon and deposit their take (in return for food). The wagon contains some 1,500gp in rat accumulated merchandise (yes, these are pack rats).

# 10) Puppets

A small puppet theater has been set up on a street corner. As the party approaches, Punch is thwacking Judy with a club.

If the party pauses to watch (this could be decided by a saving roll against the average WIS for the party), there will be a pickpocketing attempt on a random member of the group.

The [[people/puppet-show-thief|Puppet Show Thief]] (#902) is 4th level and has a pickpocketing skill of 65%. He is quite fleet and will be able to outdistance all but a loaded bow since it requires only 30 seconds for him to reach the nearest alley. If the theft is successful, the victim's purse (wallet, pouch, or what have you) will have been emptied, and all carried cash will be gone.

# 11) Warning

A youth sprints out of a nearby alley, and crashes through the party (bumping into a randomly selected player-character). He spins around, regaining his balance, then dashes into another alley and disappears entirely.

The character who was bumped into will find that a small piece of parchment has been pressed into their hand during the confusion. The message on it is in the common script and reads "Get out of town quick - [[people/zarken|Zarken]] is looking for you!"

# 12) Collection

Two goliaths ([[people/kuori-anakalathia|Kuori Anakalathia]] and [[people/vimak-anakalathia|Vimak Anakalathia]]), armed with stout cudgels (i.e."tree trunks"), approach the party from behind. They have very obviously been following the group for about 6 blocks. They select one member of the party by referring to a piece of vellum one of them carries and inform him (in very deep bass voices) that No-Yen expects his money back, that the note the character signed is already 60 days overdue, and that they intend to collect, NOW!

The piece of vellum is a line drawing of the debtor involved, which happens to closely resemble the character selected from the party. If the party is sufficiently awed by the sheer size of the goliaths and antes up the money (the amount claimed to be owed is 1d10 x 1d10 x 1d10 x 2gp), the giants will leave peaceably. If the party refuses, the giants will attempt direct seizure of the person of the “debtor", with the intention of hauling him off to [[people/no-yen-drachmah|No-Yen Drachmah]] (see North Corridor Shops). The goliaths will fight, if necessary, to accomplish their goal. There is a 35% chance that the Guard will be attracted by the ruckus, and intervene before anyone is injured seriously, arresting all parties concerned, and hustling them off to the nearest Guard barracks.

# 13) Relative?

A reasonably well-dressed person begins following the party, making no attempt to conceal their presence, or the interest they show in the group.

If the party does not turn to investigate, the person will follow the group for 2d10+10 [roll:2d10+10] minutes. Whether the party approaches the person, or the person approaches the party, they will ask one particular character many questions concerning childhood, family, relatives, etc. At some point, the well-to-do person will proclaim the character a long-lost relative (15% chance the character actually is) - from that point, the GM is on their own.

# 14) Assassin

(If encounter has not yet occurred, select another encounter) An arrow narrowly misses (or just barely hits) a member of the party. As the party searches for the hidden [[people/bowman|Bowman]] (or cowers behind cover), a cloaked figure can be spied, disappearing from a nearby building roof.

The figure is [[people/zarken|Zarken]] (#903), an assassin, mentioned in the note passed to the player in encounter. The target is the player-character who received the note (an obvious case of mistaken identity). There is a 25% chance that the arrow has struck, doing minor damage (1d4 hits 1d4). If this encounter occurs a fourth time for the same character, [[people/zarken|Zarken]] will not miss. There is an 85% chance that the shot will be fatal immediately.

# 15) Chase

A fleet-footed young person dashes through the party, with 3 guardsmen hot on his tail.

A cutpurse is fleeing the law. They weren't quite adept enough at his chosen profession yet. If the guard catches the speedy one, they will likely never become adept.

# 16) "Stallions"

A troop of young horsemen, mounted on steeds of excellent quality, thunders down the street past the party. They are wearing green swordsman's capes, secured with white brooches, and knee-boots of black leather.

These are members of the [[organizations/young-stallions|Young Stallions]]. If any of the party is nobility, and is wearing evidence of rank (emblazoned shield, coat of arms, etc.), they will rein up, and start a conversation. Otherwise, chances and saving rolls are as in encounter.

# 17) Hell-fire

A reasonably decent looking woman in her late thirties is declaiming to anyone who will listen about the iniquities of just about anything that is any fun at all. She begins to follow the party, calling them sinners, consigning them to various forms of hellfire and brimstone if they don't change their "sinful ways", etc. She cannot be driven off and will continue to make a fuss until the guard arrives (2d10 2d10 minutes), and rather unceremoniously hauls her away.

As long as the party does no actual harm to the woman, there will be no problem. If harm is done to her, the guard will arrest everybody.

# 18) Book Sale

The party is approached by [[people/ubechalyff|Ubechalyff]] (#904), a furtive, stoop shouldered man with a bushy black mustache, very thick, very mobile eyebrows, and heavy spectacles. He is wearing bulky clothing, including a coat (which is way too long for him), and has a large cigar which appears to be permanently clenched in his left fist.

As he approaches, he walks with a curious weaving, low-hipped motion. He will, amidst a barrage of useless chatter, patter, and bad jokes, make an offer to sell the party a book. The book has a black leather cover, a cover lock (locked), and is quite thick and reasonably heavy. The man claims he "found it on [[settlements/magic-street|Magic Street]], which could be significant, but then again, if you say the magic word, it could be a duck. I don't read magic, anyway." He has no key for the lock, of course.

The man will ask 1,000gp for the book. He can easily be argued down to 150gp (this may require an hour of bad puns and wise cracks, however). The book will be one of several things:

1. A big black book (with a list of, and ratings for, a lot of the girls on [[settlements/the-street-of-silk-veils|The Street of Silk Veils]].
2. A trapped demon (there is a 65% chance the demon will simply take off, glad to regain his freedom).
3. An honest-to-gods, actual, real, not-faked magic book (GM's discretion as to contents).
4. An incomplete halfling genealogy.
5. A topologically altered duck (the spell will wear off in about 3 hours, and the duck will reappear).

Once he has either sold the book or been totally rejected, the man will [[people/bob|Bob]] and weave on his way (…never to be seen again?).

# 19) Buy a Duck?

The man described in encounter Book Sale, [[people/ubechalyff|Ubechalyff]], now carrying a duck under his arm, sidles up to the party, and inquires, "Wanna buy a duck?" He claims that the duck is magical, can talk, and lays golden eggs. He is willing to sell it for only 250gp.

As it happens, the duck fulfills 2 of the 3 claims made: it is magical, and it does lay golden eggs. Every 1d4 1d4 days the duck will produce an egg, the yolk of which is pure gold, and worth 1d10gp 1d10, depending on the size of the yolk.

However, after 5d20 days 5d20, the duck will sicken, and egg production will cease. A visit to a veterinarian will provide the information that the duck is suffering from a rare disease which prevents egg laying. This disease can be arrested by feeding the duck special herbs, which can only be found in far (GM's choice of country).

# 20) Yok (Dup of)

Down the street toward the party comes a sedan chair borne by four very large and burly men. In the sedan chair sits a gnome-like man with an elongated head. As they group draws abreast of the party, they stop, and the midget inside addresses the party.

*"I am Yok, the seer, who sees all the future. I know what you wish to do. For a portion of your gains, I will help you to accomplish your objectives. Come with me."* At a signal, the sedan chair moves off down the street.

The sedan chair will be carried into an alley. If the party follows, the men who are carrying the chair will take a twisting turning path calculated to confuse anybody. Eventually it will lead to a cul-de-sac with a door at the end. Yok is a fraud. He can see the same distance into the future as anybody else - little or none. However, his burly aides (#905,,,) are accomplished muggers, who will seize the first safe opportunity (when the party is unaware) to practice their profession.

# 21) Auction

A wooden auction block and a podium have been set up in the plaza (square, wide space, etc.), and a rather listless group is bunched back of it from the crowd which is rapidly gathering. A well-dressed man approaches the podium, and motions one of the listless group up onto it. There appear to be several guards in the vicinity to maintain order among both the gathering crowd and the listless group

This is an auction for the services of indentured servants, people who have pledged their services for a number of years to pay debts of some sort. The indentured are 65% male (x 3), and the remainder female (x 5). Terms of service run from 1 to 15 years. Age for an individual indentured is 14 to 70.

To obtain the general value of an indentured contract, use the following formula:

- Value in gp = (length<sup>2</sup> x Sex Value x Age ) divided by 10

Indentured servants may be punished, if deemed necessary, but they have more rights than slaves, and may not be killed out of hand. When their term of service has expired, an indenture is free, no longer bound to the master they have served.

To conduct the auction:

The auctioneer will accept bids starting at 1% of the value of the contract (or item):

- From 1gp to 50gp, the minimum raise is 1gp (NPC competitors will increase the bid by 1d10gp)
- From 51gp to 150gp, the minimum raise is 5gp (NPCs 5 x 1d10gp)
- From 151gp to 500gp, the minimum is 10gp (NPCs - 10 x 1d10gp)
- From 501gp up, 25G (NPCs - 25 x 1d10gp)

The GM should keep track of the last amount bid

- For each call for a bid, roll the dice
- If there are three consecutive calls for a raise with no response, the auction is closed, and the contract (or item) has been sold
- If a raise is made, add the amount of the raise to the last amount bid
- If the players do not bid, the NPCs in the audience probably will

The chances for a raise in the bidding are dependent on the relation of the bid to the value of the contract

All transactions are cash on the barrel head.

# 22) [[people/mang|Mang]]

A young man, obviously excited at the thought of talking with adventurers, approaches the group and begs them to let him join their party on their next adventure. He appears eager and willing, but seems to be slightly clumsy, tripping, dropping items, etc.

This is [[people/mang|Mang]] the Neophyte (#701), the perennial tyro. He is helpful, courteous, kind, cheerful, loyal, brave, reverent, etc. (sort of a medieval boy scout). He is also very clumsy. However, in compensation, he is extremely lucky. For instance, when he trips and falls, he finds a purse with 50gp lying in front of his nose. Many of his mishaps have a happy ending (GM's discretion).

# 23) Veils

The party is approached by a bevy 1d4 of veiled women, to proposition one of the members of the group.

 The GM should refer to the GM's Notes in the section on [[settlements/the-labyrinth|The Labyrinth]] for methods and prices in situations such as this.

# 24) Trick Shot

A [[people/bowman|Bowman]] is giving an exhibition of artistry in archery. He will invite one of the party to assist him by standing as target, Mile the [[people/bowman|Bowman]] shoots a small piece of fruit from the character's mouth.

 The boman, Mile (#909), will gently rag the party a bit for cowardice if no one co-operates. The crowd surrounding the place of the exhibition will join good-naturedly in the harassment, until one of the party either gives in or leaves.

 If one of the player-characters stands as target, the GM should graphically describe the [[people/bowman|Bowman]] taking careful aim at the character's head, the drawing of the bow to its fullest extent, etc., until the player is completely positive that his character is about to be killed out of hand.

 As it happens, the [[people/bowman|Bowman]] has an enchanted bow with spells specially embedded for trick-shooting accuracy, and magical arrows which will not strike living flesh. The piece of fruit will be neatly plucked from the character's mouth, leaving him or her completely unscratched, even if they are a nervous wreck.

# 25) Urchin Gang

*(This encounter will not occur on a major or crowded street. Normally, the more deserted the street, the more likely this is to happen.)*

All at once, the party is surrounded, intercepted, infiltrated, knocked down, and pilfered by a large pack of street rats (kids, not rodents), the children quickly disappear with their loot.

 The speed and ferocity of the attack are the primary reasons for its success. Each character will be hit and attacked by 6 to 8 children, and 1d6 items of exterior equipment and apparel will be snatched off. Determine randomly, but these items should be the most valuable appearing at a quick glance (a flashy piece of costume jewelry is more likely to disappear than a magic sword with a plain hilt or pommel).

# 26) Mean Drunk

As the party passes a tavern, a [[people/drunk-swordsman|Drunk Swordsman]] staggers out and collides with a member of the group. He is in a mean mood, and immediately accuses the person he bumped into of tripping him. Unless an abject apology is forthcoming, and swiftly, the drunk whips out a rapier and challenges the party member by the simple expedient of cutting him with the sword (1d3 damage).

His purse contains 1d10 x 1d6gp [roll:1d10*1d6].

# 27) Happy Drunk

As in encounter, a drunk makes his appearance, but considers the person he bumped into to be his only friend in the whole wide world.

 The drunk will fawn, slobber, whiskey-breath, and  grope all over the poor victim. He will insist on accompanying the party but will begin singing loud (and lewd) songs (off key) as the group moves on.

 His purse contains 1d8 x 1d5gp [roll:1d8*1d5].

# 28) Dead Drunk

A well-dressed drunk staggers from a tavern, into the middle of the party, and passes out, collapsing on the ground. Three gold coins roll out of the pouch on his belt.

 Attempts to waken the drunk will be of little avail. He is out for the duration. His purse contains some 700gp in coins and small gems.

# 29) Argument

Down a side-alley, two men can be seen involved in a fierce (verbally, that is) argument regarding possession of a sack on which each of them has a partial hold. Suddenly, one of them wrenches the sack from the other's grasp, and runs up the alley, straight into the party. His compatriot starts in pursuit.

 The sack contains a small chest (locked, Complexity 3) which holds some 300gp in coins and small gems. The two are thieves who are having a falling out. If the party presents a united and menacing front to the pair. they will simply seek to escape. There is a 75% chance that the first thief will drop the sack when he runs into the party.

# 30) Carlo

A well-dressed young boy with a withered arm wanders up to the party, looks up at the apparent leader, and says "I know what you're planning. If you don't take me along, I'll tell the guardsman over there," pointing to a City Guard about 80' away.

 This is [[people/carlo-de-la-riis|Carlo de la Riis]] (#121, see the de la Riis Family). He does know what the party is planning but is willing to keep quiet if he is allowed to join in the adventure.

# 31) Kidnapping

- [[people/kidnapper-1|Kidnapper 1]]
- [[people/kidnapper-2|Kidnapper 2]]
- [[people/kidnapping-driver|Kidnapping Driver]]

The party hears a loud feminine scream. About half-a-block ahead an enclosed carriage is stopped, and a young woman is being forced to enter by three men. She is obviously reluctant and is screaming at the top of her lungs. Various passers-by ignore the furor.

 As soon as the girl has been manhandled into the carriage, it takes off in a burst of speed as the driver plies the whip to the horses. There is a 50% chance the carriage is headed toward the party (mud and injury chances as in encounter).

Gallantry (i.e., trying to rescue the girl, or to stop the carriage) by one or more members of the party should be encouraged by the GM (DEX check DC15, add 2 horsemanship) to avoid being run over by the carriage when the leap is unsuccessful.

There are two men in the carriage, who will put up a fight to keep the girl and escape with her, and the driver (#913), who will fight only to protect himself, merely attempting to escape as quickly as possible.

The girl's father will reward the party for a successful rescue (GM's discretion as to reward).

# 32) Ransoming

A very expensively clad man (obvious nobility - he is accompanied by four retainers all clad in the same livery) accosts the party. The weapons of his men are in their scabbards, and they make no hostile moves. He asks if the party is open to take a special job.

If the party shows interest in the situation, the noble explains that his youngest daughter, Jennae, was abducted about two days previously (the girl who was successfully kidnapped in encounter.). He has received a set of written instructions from the kidnappers, and feels he has no choice but to pay the ransom. He needs two neutral men to deliver the money. He is willing to pay the party handsomely (1,000gp) to deliver the ransom money to the specified place and person at the correct time. The specified place is in [[settlements/the-labyrinth|The Labyrinth]], at the corner of Oust Way and Y, a seamy tavern called [[landmarks/the-silver-penny|The Silver Penny]] (there is a 15% chance that whoever accepts the job will be mugged while attempting to accomplish the mission).

# 33) Monster Man

A heavily cloaked and cowled figure moves with a limp, haltingly, along the street. A crowd of children swirls around the figure, taunting, throwing small globs of mud and garbage, and generally making thorough going nuisances of themselves. The cloaked figure, unable to proceed further, leans against a wall. At this point a particularly well-thrown mud ball knocks his cowl back and off his head, revealing a hideous mass of mis-colored puffy growths, giving the face the appearance of a demon's mask. The figure cries piteously for help.

This is [[people/curadon|Curadon]] (#702)and he is also known as the Monster Man. He is returning from a mission to aid a special friend (Curadon is a healer of repute and power) and was caught by the pack of children. If the party aids him, helping him to return to [[landmarks/the-house-of-healing|The House of Healing]] on [[settlements/magic-street|Magic Street]], the healers of the city will be most grateful (and the gratitude of healers is nothing to scoff at).

# 34) Beggars

A miasma of 3-12 beggars 3d4 surrounds the party, crying for alms. Their sores and scabs are sickeningly displayed, and they threaten by their closeness to touch the party with this evidence of disease and foulness.

 Beggars (sample beggar,) are a common occurrence in any major city, particularly in the poorer sections. They will usually be satisfied with an average of 2d4cp 2d4 for each beggar in the group.

 There is a 35% chance they will call blessings upon the party if an adequate donation is made, or a 90% chance they will rain curses and occasionally garbage on their proposed almoners if nothing is forthcoming.

 If a beggar makes physical contact with a person, there is a 40% chance of contracting fleas, and a 5% chance of contracting some contagious disease. It is generally deemed best to keep this human garbage at a reasonable distance either by bribing them with coins, or by driving them off with weapons.

# 35) Crier

A chunky man with a loud, penetrating voice strolls down the street, crying out various taglines and headlines to local news stories. Every so often, someone will go up to him, deposit a coin in his hand, and listen carefully to what he tells them.

 This is the local crier, peddling news stories and gossip all over the town. His charges are usually in the range of 1 or 2 bits for local news or gossip, 1 copper for a major story, and as much as 1gp for a story of earthshaking importance. He is an excellent source of general information for the players and their characters regarding current and upcoming events, and the direction of the political winds. There is a crew of approximately thirty of these men wandering the town.

# 36) [[people/astrologer|Astrologer]]

A tall skinny man in a robe elaborately decorated with symbols of sun, stars, moons, and planets runs, panting, up to the party. He points to one of the group and gasps out "You are destined for great things! I have seen it in the stars! Come with me."

The [[people/astrologer|Astrologer]] (#915) will lead the party to his shop, Mere he will pour out a story about great portents he has seen in the patterns of the stars. He will explain that he needs 1d10 x 150gp for the materials to make a thorough study of the matter, so that he can fully determine the future.

Most of the time, this is a total come-on. The old man is needs to raise cash and is simply attempting to sucker the party into supplying him with some funds. There is, however, a 5% chance that the [[people/astrologer|Astrologer]] is on the level (if he is, the GM gets to devise the "momentous happening" that involves the party).

# 37) Crotofiran

A [[people/friar|Friar]] in a brown robe with a red sash hails the group and asks for a contribution to "feed and clothe the poor little children."

The [[people/friar|Friar]] (#916) is a member of the [[organizations/religious/monks-of-saint-crotofiros|Monks of Saint Crotofiros]]. It is considered good luck to contribute, however small it may be, and bad luck to interfere with one of these men or harm them in any way. If the players contribute, there is a 35% chance that things will go particularly well for a period. If the players should commit some atrocity on the priest (don't laugh, GMs, you've seen it happen before), apply the Murphy Principle heartily (if it can go wrong…it does!) to anything the players attempt for about a game year (or until the characters involved are all deceased).

# 38) Hot Priest
