## Description

Within this huge roofed area are conducted the daily auctions of stock for which The [[settlements/the-south-corridor|The South Corridor]] is justly renowned. The Block itself is a podium located in a ring in the center of the building, surrounded by a set of bleachers where sit the bidders. Bids are made by voice response, for the chief auctioneer, Fast-talkin Eddie Schultz, is going blind, and can no longer see the signals flashed by the patrons. The Block is a noisy place, particularly when a good lot is being offered, as the shouted bids fly thick and fast. There are seldom fewer than 60-80 people in attendance on the days horses are offered, although there are sometimes less than 20 on the varied stock days.
