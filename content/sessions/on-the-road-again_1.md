But there is a commotion going on at the Georgeson mansion/town hall. She says she is headed across the street to see what is going on. Martin and Penelope go with her.

There are people standing around, and the door to the town hall seems to have been broken open from the outside. It looks like it has been shouldered open.

Florin asks why it is broken down. A guy comes from inside and says they are tired of not knowing what is going on so they decided to find out. They did and he's dead. In his sons room, just dead.

The three ofs go to the son's room. Georgeson is curledp at the foot of the bed. Florin checks him and says he's dead.

The body is cool to the touch and shows no signs of external injury. Florin goes to make some arrangements. Penelope brings the body over to the temple.

We hear the muttering about magic, werewolves, grief, etc. We decide to take an hour to just look around to see if we can figure out what might have happened. In the residence side of the house, we find a few strange things. Toys strewn about, water dripped on a painting of the boy (which tastes vaguely salty). The boy's bed is pristine, as if waiting for the boy to come home.

The cupboard looksntouched, like he hasn't been eating. Eugene examines the body and finds nothing.

With nothing else to do, we head out of town.

We approach Diggy. He says we should talk to Cham who is next to him. So Martin turns to Cham and says that his acquaintance Diggy told him to talk to her.

Cham says the mayor opened the mine a few months ago, and there was a call for miners so the four of them heeded the call. There was a monster in the mine that killed Jackie and the others. It was a mess. They got out, and the mayor closed it off. This was a week or so ago.

None of them had seen the monster. They heard screams but saw nothing. The monster made a basic roar. They were mining iron, but they heard rumors theresed to be gems in the mine.

If we want to help we should talk to Gingar Terion. He sometimes comes in for lunch, or we could go to his house out of town. Martin suggests Arada should negotiate our fee as she is better at haggling.

After a little while, a distinguished gentleman appears at the door and goes to the bar then retires into a side room.
