Dawn breaks. Nothing exciting seems to have happened overnight. We need to wrap thisp by the afternoon if we want to catchp with the barge. Over breakfast, Eugene looks to identify Arada's vest. He can tell it is imbued with very powerful conjuring magic, but it is hard to tell. They discuss how Arada hassed it in the past.

We ask Andreas to watch the horses, and to stay safe, but also to guard them with his life. He points out those two things are mutually exclusive.

We discuss exploring one of the towers before we go back into the manor, just so we don't get surprised from behind. The roof is gone, but it looks like it would provide shelter from the wind, and be a good place to light a campfire that wouldn't be seen. We find a large wheel of cheese, and a jug of something that seems like coarse flour. Martin tastes it, and it seems to be a mixture of flour and ground bone. We take the cheese, and leave the boneflour. Except a tiny pouch Eugene takes.

We find a room off a hallway we didn't go down before. It looks like a shrine of worship that has been turned into living space. There are three segments to the room. Built in benches are part of it. To the west is a pulpit, and to the south are stairs that gop. On the pulpit, Turin notices the pages of the book are turning by themselves.

Arada finds herself in a T intersection. To the west it opens into a larger room with tables and chairs. To the east she sees it opens into another large room and she hears heavy breathing. She comes back to the group.

Meanwhile in a room halfwayp a set of stairs, Eugene sees an ogre. A really big ogre. Turin closes and grabs the book. He hears an angry squeak from nearby. He sees a small and demonic thing, so he shoots it.

Eugene webs the ogre into place. We'll see if it works. It does not.

The ogre comes in and swings it's great staff and bashes Penelope. Penelope bashes it back. Her sword glows softly and it heals her. Turin fires three arrows, hitting it three times. Arada runs in, but misses. Martinses sacred flame, and the ogre falls down. Eugene looks at the staff, and it appears there are magical charms hanging on it.

Eugene also gets a wand that allows him to detect magic. Heses it and notices the book is faintly magic, the charms on the greatstaff are magic, and nothing else seems to be.

More ogres appear. We need to burn the spawn nest or something.

We look around the room we were in. They appear to be fancy quarters for an ogre. One ogre had a few coins. Obviously the leader.
